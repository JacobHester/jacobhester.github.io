Design Principles in Mobile Sites

Proximity —

Www.typeform.com is well organized and and has great proximity. The spacing in between links and headings is well thought out and is easy to read. They have decent sized buttons and links to make mobile viewing fairly easy. They have good examples of using white space and keep their website simple and free of clutter.

Alignment —

Alignment make the website appealing, when we look at this website, we like it .How it is aligned the text ,photos in this website make it pleasant for our eyes. We want to spend more time in this website. In this website is easy to scan the page , everything is well organized , search bar is in the corner right , on opposite side is BBC , so it is well aligned .That is well designed and make it , so is easy to find it things we are serching.On top we also see other categories that make it easy to navigate.On the middle is picture that takes our attention Alignment makes a good reading experience , photos and text are well aligned .There is little text and it is place in the midle and the right which is easy to read.

Repetition —

lds.org

I thought LDS.org was a good example of repetition. Repetition is when you repeat the same element of design throughout the web page. The first thing you see is videos one right after the other. This is a good job at repeating the same graphic type over and over again. Each video has a blue line on it then under it is the title of each video. The titles are all in the same font and are bold face to unify all the pieces together. This web site shows consistency.

Contrast —

craftsman.com

The principle that the Craftsman website demonstrates is contrast. The types of contrast that it exhibits are color, typeface, size and alignment. As I searched over different sites I could choose Apple.com stuck out due to its clear and crisp typography.
